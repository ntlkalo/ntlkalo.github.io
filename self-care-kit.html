DIY Self Care Kit

Caring for myself is not self-indulgence, it is self-preservation, and that is an act of political warfare. ~ Audre Lorde

Congratulations! You've decided to make a self care kit for yourself or a loved one. Good on you! However, it is strongly encouraged that the self care kit addresses physical, mental, and emotional needs.

Physical: Sometimes we feel icky because we're tired, hungry, or dehydrated. Perhaps we can't summon up the energy to address our physical needs, or we just need a quick reminder. Some ideas are sleep aids like a sleeping mask, sleepy time tea, or a pillow spray. Or a prompt to drink water, do yoga, or go outside.

If the issue is physical discomfort, travel sized pain killers, icy hot patches, and/or a video to self-massage could be just what the doctor ordered.

Personal hygiene items like deorderant, face wipes, or a change of underwear are also popular choices.

Mental: We all have bad days where it seems self-evident that we are abysmal failures. When our problem-solving skills are focused on tearing ourselves down, we need to meet it with some good old-fashioned logic. self compassion worksheets are shown to improve mood.

Emotional: Unfortunatley, we're not purely rational creatures. We know that our bad mood is a dark cloud and it too will pass, but it still sucks. So be sure to include some comfort foods and objects to wait out the storm. A lot of rainy day favorites (e.g. comic books, books, favorite movies) also work for our inner gray days as well.

White noise emulators like Rainymood and Coffitivtiy can help your environment feel more calming.
